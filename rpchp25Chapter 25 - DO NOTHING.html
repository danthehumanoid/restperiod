Chapter 25 - DO NOTHING

For four days he sits. He doesn’t lift a single weight. He sloths, sleeps, play videos games and watches tv. I don’t know exactly what he does, but his coach told him to do nothing.

And when he returns, he’s stronger and more powerful than ever. This man is strong, deceptlt strong. He trains as if he’s going to war.

Barbell squats plates on plates, does handstands and aggressively jumps with every single repetition. His programs are brutal, its high load, sometimes 3 hour sessions. And when it comes time to deload and rest between programs, his instructions are clear.

“Do nothing”.

I’m not even sure if he even puts clothes on during this period. I might in fact be exaggerating his effort of nothing tinder to recover. But knowing this person well enough, I know he also loves to be at home and do quote in quote “nothing”. And it’s the most productive nothing.

Everyone who’s training in high performance realms needs rest, recovery, tapers and deloads. And everyone responds differently. And depending on the type of sport, training block and individual, these will always vary.

I was most fascinated by this althete as I had, along with a few other clients had the experience of coming back feeling stronger after a small sickness.

Say a cold that last 2-3 days, forcing you to back off movement and take it slower. Maybe it even wipes you for 48 hours. Then you come back in your training and surprise yourself with a heavy lift, more explosive jump or a feeling of freshness.

Yes, a sweet sweet replenished nervous system. This, is was it feels like to be fully recovered Begs the question, could you benefit from not just some lighter training session to recover, but to do absolutely nothing?
