Chapter 30 - LET THE BODY DO THE THING

“Do I think about squeezing my quads when I run Dan?”.

“If you want to, but how are you going to think about running whilst running?”

During an 8 kilometer tempo run, I tried an experiment for every step of my run. I wanted to get more muscle activation in my feet. Everysingle step i took , I focused on nothing but pushing down with my big toes. “Big toe push, big toe push”. For over 7000 steps that’s what I kept bringing my focus back to. It was a fun run, I thought I had made some great progress. Two hours later the calf tightness hit me. I was limping for 3 days after the run. Tight, sore and unable to walk upstairs or downstairs properly. It took 4 weeks to fully recover, and as I got back into running, I managed a small tear in one of my calves.

I didn’t let the body do the thing.

At the end of a lecture, the teacher condlued that talk by reminding us that when we are ni the lab (the gym) training, we are setting ourselves up with the ability to do other things i.e. our sports better. And when we are playing sport, the main thing we want to focus on is doing the sport. I took a training cue from my program, injected it straight into my run and multiplying the TUT (time under tension) by 1000. These exercises are far better practiced in isolation rather than 7000 times during a run.

When we work on our body in the gym, it’s worth reminding ourselves that at some point we need to let go of the cues, the control and the idea that we should be thinking about muscle activation all of the time. Maybe you do for a while. The goal is for those things to be automatic, default behaviour.

Move from conscious competence to unconscious competence, it take far less energy.

Building confidence isn’t always about total control of our bodies movements. As we start to unlock new abilities of strength, speed and coordination, letting go and trusting our body’s to find the best way to do things is powerful.

You’ve created the foundations, built resilience, done the work. Now let your body do the sport, dance move, balance drill or THING
