Chapter 32 - DON’T WIN, COMPETE

Hamish Blake, a body building legend.

Hamish Blake, an australian comedian once entered a New York Body Building Championship. Unprepared, not conditioning for the sport, somewhat spray tanned and with no training he won the heavyweight devision. Crowned a champion he said his success came down to the reality that “Sometimes you just turn up and automatically win, because no-one else showed up”. He came first in the heavyweight division in a class where there was noone else competing but himself.

TTC, a 2022 goal. I had ticked off enough strength markers and started running again. Now it was time to test myself. Who could I beat? What did being the best mean? Where could I go? I also wasn’t anywhere near strong enough to be considered the best or strongest at anything, in any weight grade or division?

I could enter a local lifting competition and probably podium. But that’s because there would likely be 3-4 people in my category.

I could enter that trail run race and get in the top 10 for my age group. But what if noone shows up and I win? What if every competitive 30 year old runner shows up and I get smoked?

Hanging successes on winning is an attitude that has been built into many os us from an early age. It mattered for my rugby team as soon as the first child in our team learned to count.

“Did we win Coach?”

“It was a draw kids”

“No! We scored 5 more tries than them! The score was 45-20!”

“I guess you’re right, ok I’m counting the score from now on.”

Winning has become an attitude of many in sports, business and life. To win means beating other people. And we all have a person in our lives who lives and dies by that mantra. Many of us love to play to win. Some of us don’t know where the line is.

“Fastest dishwasher load of the year, beat you all family! Where’s my trophy, my framed certificate, my congratulations and recognition 10 years later?!”

As Robbie Williams sand is his song “Losers” - “There will always be someone better than you, so lets stop the competition now.
