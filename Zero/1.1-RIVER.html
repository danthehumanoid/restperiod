The River

Riley pulled up to the river, the wind had settled slighty.

She could see the river moving fast, but it was still shallow, shallow enough that she could walk to her usual spot in the middle.

Slightly wet feet, (she didn't care, nothing lasts forever) she stood in the middle of the river, on dry rocks.

New rocks.

Her rocks, but only for now.

She quickly found a round flat rock, the size of her palm and threw it lightly along the water. It skimmed three times then dissappeared. Her second throw sank without a single skim (as it always did!). She took a deep breathe, her vision expanded. She had landed. Her mind was where her body was. Connected to her surroundings, she picked up a third rock, threw it a littler harder than the first two and it grazed the waters surface almost to the other side of the water. It stopped aburtly this time hitting something sticking out of the water. It had struck something hard, metal like. From a distance, it looked like a metal pipe.

Curious, she threw another rock without hitting the water she connected and it made the same "dong", this time a little louder. She tried to guess what note it sounded most simolar to. She opted for a G2 to be exact (or completly wrong).

The light began to fade from the sky, Riley was hungry, but also curious.

She removed her shoes, and wet socks, and waded out to the object that was sticking out of the water. The river's current was strong, but she knew her limits and carried on as the water went up to her knees. She lifted her dress slightly, then gave up as the water went up to her thighs. The sound of the road was now drowned out by the rushing river's water and her focus on the object that was now infront of her.

Riley stuck her hand in the water and pulled out the metal object, it was a sign. Engraved on a piece of rusted metal. Still readable despite it's patina. She removed some sticks and branches from it to reveal a quote.

"My income, life, friends and identity doesn't depend on being me right, or ignorant"

Riley read it again.

She turn the metal piece over and saw something that resembled a dial. Around the dial were the words "hedonist, eudomonia, spite, sadist, empathy and a few others that she couldnt quite make out
