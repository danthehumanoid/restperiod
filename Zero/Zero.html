"You take any note and count up by three, then four."

That's how you make a major chord.

And a sad one, you flip the 3 and four, you have yourself a minor."

"I wish i knew this 10 years ago" Riley processed this information.

Every piano was a work of art. There was always some signifacnt effort to build eveything you saw, and couldnt see. No matter how cheap, someone sweated over this. If even to lift one!

"Thanks Jo, see you next week?"

"Sure, and remember, play the keys like you're holding two hamsters"

She could only imagine a hamster on a wheel, not in his hands. Gueinea pigs would do.

Riley left Jo's house, walked out onto the pavement, took a breath and saw a fire engine shoot past on the road he was about to cross. They always looked so calm inside the cabin.

"Calm is contagious" she remembered in a book she flicked through. And sometimes annoying. Even if it makes sense.

Riley continued back to her car, she had left her drivers window down, gym bag on the passengers seat, laptop underneath.

"Nothing taken", a small win for humanity.

Riley started her car, and noticed that her cars idle sound resemebled a chord she had been practicing; D# minor.

As she wound her window up, she made a rough guess that it sounded like a B major.

The wind was picking up outside and the streets were clear.

"Maybe I'll drive to the river", throw some rocks and see if I can hit the bank on the other side again".

Riley began pulling out, checking behind her car she slammed her brakes as a motorcyclist sped past her, narroly missing her drivers door. She felt her heart jump.

"No crash, no accident, I'm fine". Her heart still continued to beat fast. Faster than she felt comforatble. The wind contnuted to whistle outside.

She thought about going home now instead of the river.
