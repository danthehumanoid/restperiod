Riely pulled up to her driveway. A cat lay in the driveway, rolling on the concrete stopping her from pulling all the way in. She waited, until eventually she was able to roll into her car park, number 42

Number 42, Apartment 42, on the second floor.

Riley opened the fridge. Left overs looked appealing, but for some reasons she craved something else.

She grabbed her swipe card and took the stairs back down to her car.

She opted to walk, and saw the same cat again. This time rolling in the car park next to her. She double took as she spotted something slightly off. Was the cat having a siezure? It seemed stuck in the same loop. Over and over again. She watched for at least a few minutes, the same 5 second loop of rolling on the concrete.

Was she that hungry and dilerious?

She decided to approach the cat to try and help it. Maybe she could calm it, watching wasnt helping. As she approached, she felt a tingling around her body, a warmth of electrccity and something similar to euphoria but not quite.

Her hand reached out to touch the cat. As her fingers brushed the fur, Riley jottled herself back as she felt herself experiencing a heap of flashbacks.

She reached out again, this time fully touching the cat. She felt the electrical warmth swatm her body and pull her forwards.

Riley felt herself spiralling in a vortex of mater, she felt like a tiny dot amongst an enourmous amount of energy. She could see two small picutres infront of her which looked like glass snow globes, each with two moving images would she recognised. The river she frequently visited and her piano teachers studio.

She felt another pull and reached forwards to grab one of the globes
