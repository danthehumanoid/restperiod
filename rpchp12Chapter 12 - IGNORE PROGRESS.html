Chapter 12 - IGNORE PROGRESS

The scientist in me says, how dare you. The mover in me says “whatever I’m having fun!

How many reps did you do on the dance floor? How many steps was your hike with your close friends? How many times did you hit the ball in the court whilst playing tennis? Sometimes metrics don’t matter and the workout isn’t important. The enjoyment of movement is the most important part.

To climb a mountain only to check your heart rate before taking in the view is missing the main reason to climb a mountain.

Loosen the grip on your need to measure everything through a lens of numbers. The feeling of what you’ve done matters. You’ve moved your body, got out of your head, shared a laugh (2x10 second laughs with a 2 minute rest in between) and had fun. Maybe you didn’t even warm up properly, who cares?!

This is the unpredictability and beauty of movement, spontaneous or otherwise. Leave the smart watch at home, trust your instinct to know when to stop and take the chance to enjoy your body, without thinking “Am I Getting better?”. Know when to buckle up, put your head down and work hard. Also know, and make a regular practice to let go, move your body, play and enjoy movement.

That’s what many of us did as kids before we were taught to compete.

Remind yourself that both are available.
