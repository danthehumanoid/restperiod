Chapter 3 - LOW BUY IN

When I was working at a Big Box gym, we had shared locker rooms with other staff and trainers. Over the course of 10 years everyone became quite familiar with everyone’s pre and post workout routines; change clothes, check emails, scroll, some sort of caffeinated liquid, custom playlist and so on.

One day a trainer walked in frantically searching through their bag looking for something, as if they had lost the keys to their car.

“I don’t have any pre-workout powder left and my headphones are flat.

I can’t train today”.

Workout cancelled, progress paused.

Everybody has a workout routine. And sometimes it works against us.

For some it’s an elaborate process of getting everything aligned so as to make it the perfect session.

And of course a lot of the time this matters.

If you’re going for a run, you want your running shoes, not your squat shoes.

If you’re going swimming, you’ll need your goggles.

And if you’re just about to go mountain biking, you’ll need of course your bike.

But there are some instances where the routine becomes the barrier. Needing everything to perfectly align just to start a training session can be a dealbreaker. The perfect workout gear, charged headphones and a caffeine hit. It risks becoming a crutch.

In my experience the less you need to organise, the better. If all you need is a good meal, some water and half decent workout clothing to train in, you’re chances of training remain higher than those that need a solar eclipse in order to do cardio.
