Chapter 34 - EIGHT DAYS A WEEK

Monday, chest, Tuesday Bis and tris, Wednesday legs, Thursday rest. Rinse, repeat and skip legs every other Wednesday.

My first introduction to structured programming was in the form of the “Bro Split”. Warm up on the cross trainer, no matter what body part you’re training, Pump put a few reps with the barbell, slap some plates on and do three sets of ten followed by drop sets and set til failure. Finish every session with abs, especially side obliques.

The ultimate bodybuilding program. And for many people it actually works, for building muscle. But once you move out of newly training to become a “cloud”, things can get complicated. Fotting in running, jumping, climbing, weightlifting and more in a week sometimes doesn’t work. And sometimes things need to move around. For some, a 14 day program suits to get everything in well. For others, like myself I’ve found 10 days has worked well, although if you’re a Monday to Friday park run, Sunday sport weekend warrior, you might herniated a brain vessel trying to follow a 10 day routine. The best approach is the one that works for you. You might have a non negotiable run on a Tuesday or a game on Saturday, or you might have the freedom to plan out an entire year of training. You might get sick for two days, have a work trip or mass sleep deprivation from a new born baby. The art of scheduling in training sessions never ends. You might chose to change a training session on the day better your tired, or fresh and full of energy, ready for the biggest session of your life. It could be wet outside and you melt in the rain, or it might be the first sunny day in two weeks so you’re going to run outside no matter what.

Routine is helpful and structure is key to train if effectively. Knowing how you operate, what cadence suits you will help get you through the unforseen events, holidays, sportiness of life and at times make it seem like you’ve created the mythical 8th day in a week.
