Chapter 4 - NO MATTER WHAT

Chapter 5 - WHAT WOULD AN ATHLETE DO? I was first introduced to the concept of the athlete mindset with a coach who worked with me in my late twenties. I was feeling far from athletic. A broken ex rugby union player who had to be so injured as to cause some form of ego death to start again. Feeling open minded (finally!) and willing to try something new, I was introduced for the first time into the world of high performance… although the first training programs didn’t look like it! I spent much of my time holding isometrics, doing post pregnancy like core activation drills and countless amounts of calf raises. The coaching was simple. Turn up, Do the work and trust the process. Simple, not easy and not life changing in the short term. This attitude extends to the greatest sportspeople in the world. We only see a tiny fraction of what it has taken to achieve their world class abilities. With more and more social media training montages, we get a better glimpse of the weekly effort required to not just even improve, but maintain the conditioning required. Although freakish genetics, an entire team of coaches, therapists and trainers helping you to perform at your best might not be available to everyone, the ability to realise that every time you turn up and train with purpose, focus and block out the distraction of everything else you could be doing is available to anyone. Whether it’s in your home studio, the gym, the track or the field, you just need to show up. What would Jesus an athlete do?
