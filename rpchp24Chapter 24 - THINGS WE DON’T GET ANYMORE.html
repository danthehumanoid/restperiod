Chapter 24 - THINGS WE DON’T GET ANYMORE

Automatic gears, paddle shifters, no stick, Sensors that get the water flowing, brute strength is no longer a trick.

Cordless vacuum, stick vacuum, robot vacuum cleaner. Easy slide windows, no jamming. At least the materials are greener.

Sensor lights, button lights, automated light Scan cards, swipe cards, no room for stars at nigh

t. Run flat tires, no strength or knowledge required, Just a machine that does is it all, have a coffee, you're probably tired.

Keyless entry, push button start, no handbrake to click out. A body that now pushes, gestures, waves and orders and occasionally shouts.

Give me a pickle jar to open, or a tap to turn A heavy bucket of something to carry, Or at least something to learn.

Even a light switch to flick, or a cable to pull, I'd like to use my body, it has needs to fulfil.

I'm not looking to hunt, or start my own farm, I'm just missing small movements, like lifting my arm.

Take me from seated to standing, or running on grass. I'd love to climb with this body, and fold it in half.

Let me touch the ceiling then my toes for no good reason. Because I'm committed to move my body, throughout any season.

My surroundings are safe, and comfortable to rest. But I need to move this body, I want to feel my best.

We'll all wear out one day, hopefully in an even fashion. So we may as well enjoy our self, and embrace being a human with a passion.
